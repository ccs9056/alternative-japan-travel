Beyond Kyoto & Osaka

Onsen towns, pilgrimage trails, and temple stays. The Kansai beyond the Golden Route.

The Second Trip

Your Base in Kansai

Base in Osaka. Budget 4-5 days for this region. The Kansai Wide Area Pass covers limited express trains to every destination on this page. If you've done Kyoto's temples and Osaka's street food, this is the next layer: onsen towns, a UNESCO pilgrimage trail, temple stays in the mountains, and quiet coastline. The food shifts as you leave the city. Ryokan kaiseki in the onsen towns, Buddhist vegetarian on the mountaintop, fresh seafood on the coast.

Stay Overnight

Where to Base Beyond the Cities

Your base is Osaka. Onsen towns, mountain temples, pilgrimage trails, and quiet coast within day-trip or overnight range.

2 hrs from Osaka

Koyasan

Sleep in a Temple, Wake Before Dawn

Sleep in a 400-year-old temple. Shojin ryori (Buddhist vegetarian) for dinner, futon on tatami, morning prayer at dawn. Walk Okunoin cemetery before 7am: 200,000 tombstones under ancient cedars, yours alone before the tour buses arrive.

1 night recommended
2.5 hrs from Osaka

Kinosaki Onsen

Yukata Evenings & Seven Public Baths

Check in, change into a yukata, and spend the evening wandering between seven public baths with a wristband pass. Your ryokan handles the rest: kaiseki dinner, futon setup, and a breakfast that justifies the early alarm. 2.5 hours from Osaka by limited express.

1–2 nights recommended
3–4 hrs from Osaka

Kumano Kodo

Pilgrimage Trails & Forest Ryokan

One of two pilgrimage routes with dual UNESCO status. The Nakahechi route runs through cedar forests between ancient shrines, with village ryokan along the way. Luggage transfers between trailheads are available, so you walk with a daypack while your bags move ahead.

2–3 nights recommended
1.5 hrs from Osaka

Yoshino

Japan's Sacred Mountain of Cherry Blossoms

A mountain temple town with 30,000 cherry trees, shojin ryori dining, and hiking trails through sacred forest. The temples here predate the tourism. Yoshino feels like a pilgrimage town because it is one. If your timing lines up with late March to mid-April, the blossoms are extraordinary, but the town works year-round.

1–2 nights recommended
2 hrs from Kyoto

Amanohashidate

One of Japan's Three Scenic Views

A pine-covered sandbar across Miyazu Bay, one of Japan's official Three Scenic Views. Every Japanese person knows it; almost no international visitor comes. The tradition: bend over and view it upside-down between your legs. Onsen ryokan on the bay, fresh crab in season. 2 hours from Kyoto by limited express.

1–2 nights recommended
2.5 hrs from Osaka

Shirahama

White Sand Beach Meets Hot Springs

White sand beach on the Wakayama coast with an oceanside rotenburo (outdoor bath) at the end of the boardwalk. Soak in clifftop hot springs overlooking the Pacific. Sandanpeki Cliffs, Engetsu Island, and Adventure World (Japan's best panda facility) are all within reach. 2.5 hours from Osaka by limited express.

2 nights recommended
