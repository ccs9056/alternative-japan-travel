The Japanese Alps

Soba, Hida beef, sake breweries, and castle towns in the mountains between Tokyo and Kyoto.

The Second Trip

Your Base in the Alps

Kanazawa is your hub. 2.5 hours from Tokyo on the Hokuriku Shinkansen, 2 hours from Kyoto on the Thunderbird. Budget 4-5 days for this region. The Takayama-Hokuriku Tourist Pass covers the main circuit. This is the interior of Japan: castle towns, soba country, Hida beef, sake breweries, and onsen villages connected by mountain rail lines between Tokyo and Kyoto.

Where to Base in the Alps

Kanazawa as your hub, plus mountain towns, castle cities, and onsen villages across the Alps.

2.5 hrs from Tokyo

Kanazawa

Japan's Other Cultural Capital

Samurai districts, geisha quarters, Kenrokuen (one of Japan's three great gardens), and Omicho Market for the freshest sushi outside Hokkaido. Kanazawa was spared wartime bombing, so the old quarters are genuinely old. Two to three nights lets you settle in. The crowds are growing, but nothing close to Kyoto yet.

2 hrs from Kanazawa

Takayama

The Edo-Era Mountain Town

Edo-period streetscapes, Hida beef grilled on magnolia leaves, and sake breweries you can walk between. The morning markets open at 6am. Day trips from here: Shirakawago (50 min by bus), Kamikochi, and the Okuhida onsen valley are all within reach.

2 nights recommended Browse Stays
2 hrs from Kanazawa

Matsumoto

Castle Town at the Alpine Gateway

Matsumoto Castle is one of Japan's twelve original castles, and the black-and-white exterior against the Alps is the postcard shot. The city has a strong craft and arts scene, plus soba restaurants that rival Nagano's. From here: bus to Kamikochi (60 min) or train to the Nakasendo walking route.

1–2 nights recommended Browse Stays
1 hr from Kanazawa

Nagano

Zenkoji Temple & Alpine Gateway

Zenkoji temple is 1,400 years old. The morning prayer ceremony at dawn is open to everyone. Nagano's soba is some of the best in Japan: the buckwheat is local. Day trips to the Snow Monkeys (45 min by bus), Togakushi shrine (60 min), and Obuse's Hokusai museum (30 min by train). A base, not a transfer point.

2 nights recommended Browse Stays
20 min from Kanazawa

Toyama

The Sea-and-Mountains Gateway

Toyama Bay sushi is among the best in Japan: the fish comes straight from the deep bay to the counter. Starting point for the Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route (open April to November). 1% of international tourists visit Toyama Prefecture. If you're doing the Alps and skipping Toyama, you're missing the food.

1–2 nights recommended Browse Stays
2 hrs from Kanazawa

Nozawa Onsen

The Village That Hasn't Changed

Thirteen free public baths maintained by the village for centuries. Each one different: hot, scalding, sulfur, clear. Powder skiing in winter, onsen eggs boiled in the source water. Wooden ryokan on narrow stone streets. Bus from Nagano Station, about 90 minutes.
