Northern Japan

Tohoku and southern Hokkaido — where the bullet train ends and the real discoveries begin.

The Second Trip

Your Base in the North

Northern Japan has two hubs: Sendai for Tohoku, Sapporo for Hokkaido.

Sendai is 90 minutes from Tokyo by Shinkansen, ¥11,000. Business hotels near the station run ¥5,000-8,000/night. Famous for gyutan: beef tongue, charcoal-grilled, thicker cuts than Tokyo. Budget 3-4 days for the city and day trips.

Sapporo: fly, don't train. Domestic flights from Tokyo, ¥5,000-15,000, 90 minutes. Hotels near Susukino run ¥8,000-15,000/night. Famous for miso ramen, fresh seafood, and Hokkaido dairy. Budget 4-5 days including day trips.

Where to Base in the North

Two hub cities to base yourself, plus onsen towns, harbor cities, and castle towns worth the side trip.

90 min from Tokyo

Sendai

Your Tohoku Hub

Tohoku's largest city and your base for the north. Gyutan (beef tongue) is the signature: charcoal-grilled, thicker cuts than Tokyo, served with barley rice and tail soup. Day trips to Yamadera (60 min, mountain temple carved into a cliff) and Matsushima (40 min, island-dotted bay with temple walks). Business hotels near the station run around ¥8,000/night.

Sapporo

Your Hokkaido Hub

Hokkaido's capital and the food hub of the north. Miso ramen, fresh crab, seafood donburi, and Hokkaido dairy. Susukino is the food and nightlife district. Day trips to Otaru (35 min, canal town and sushi street) and Noboribetsu onsen (75 min). Fly from Tokyo: ¥8,000-20,000, 90 minutes. Hotels near Susukino run ¥8,000-15,000/night.

Hakodate

Port City, Morning Market, Midnight Views

The morning market opens before dawn: squid pulled live from the tank, uni bowls, seafood donburi for breakfast. The ropeway night view is one of Japan's best, and the observation deck empties by 9pm. The Meiji-era Western quarter has brick warehouses converted to shops and restaurants. One night minimum; two to eat properly.

3h 10m from Tokyo

Aomori & Hirosaki

Castle Towns at the Edge of Honshu

Hirosaki, 35 minutes inland from Aomori, has one of Japan's original castles surrounded by 2,600 cherry trees. The Nebuta Museum in Aomori city houses the massive illuminated festival floats year-round. Apple country: Hirosaki produces more apples than anywhere in Japan. Even without the blossoms, this is a two-night stay.

75 min from Sapporo

Noboribetsu

Hokkaido's Hell Valley & Volcanic Hot Springs

Boardwalks through sulfur vents and boiling mud pools, with onsen hotels fifteen minutes away. The baths are fed directly by the volcanic springs beneath them: different minerals, different colors, different temperatures. The main street gets busy. The volcanic hiking trails behind it don't. Day trip from Sapporo (75 min) or stay one night.

3 hrs from Tokyo

Nyuto Onsen

Forest Bathing in Akita's Hidden Hot Springs

Eight ryokan scattered through a mountain forest in Akita, connected by walking trails. Each has its own spring: milky white sulfur at Tsurunoyu, clear and iron-rich at Ganiba. Soak at one, dry off on the trail, soak at the next. Tsurunoyu is the most famous; book months ahead. Bus from Tazawako Station, about 45 minutes.
