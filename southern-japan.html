Southern Japan

Volcanic onsen, port cities shaped by foreign trade, and the best pork in Japan. Kyushu and Shikoku.

The Second Trip

Your Base in the South

Fly to Fukuoka from Tokyo (90 min) or Shinkansen from Osaka (2.5 hrs). Budget 5-7 days for this region. The Northern Kyushu Rail Pass connects Fukuoka to every destination on this page. Kyushu is volcanic: the onsen are fed by active geology, the port cities were shaped by centuries of foreign trade, and the food runs on pork: tonkotsu, kurobuta, mentaiko. Matsuyama and Naoshima are technically Shikoku, reachable from Hiroshima by ferry.

Where to Base in the South

Fukuoka as your hub, plus volcanic onsen, port cities, art islands, and Japan's oldest bathhouse across Kyushu and Shikoku.

90 min flight from Tokyo

Fukuoka

Your Kyushu Hub

Kyushu's largest city and the best street food in Japan. The yatai stalls along the river serve hakata ramen, yakitori, and gyoza from open-air counters every evening. Hakata Station connects to everything on this page via the Northern Kyushu Rail Pass (¥11,000/5 days). Business hotels near the station run around ¥8,000/night.

1.5 hrs from Fukuoka

Nagasaki

Where Japan Met the World

The port where Portuguese, Dutch, and Chinese traders shaped a city with no equivalent in Japan. The Atomic Bomb Museum is as powerful as Hiroshima's, with a fraction of the visitors. Dejima's trading post, Japan's oldest Chinatown, and champon noodles (a Nagasaki original). The tram covers the whole city for ¥140 per ride.

2–3 nights recommended Browse Stays
1h 20m from Fukuoka

Kagoshima

Living with a Volcano

Sakurajima volcano is 15 minutes by ferry from downtown, and it erupts regularly. The food is among Kyushu's best: kurobuta pork tonkatsu, sweet potato shochu, and Satsuma cuisine. Ash dusts the streets sometimes; the locals barely notice.

2 nights recommended Browse Stays
2 hrs from Fukuoka

Beppu

Japan's Wildest Onsen Town

More thermal water than anywhere else in Japan. Eight 'hells' for gawking (boiling pools, mud, and steam in every color), dozens of public baths for soaking, and sand baths where you get buried on the beach. Rough around the edges, full of surprises, and 100-yen public baths on every block.

2 nights recommended Browse Stays
70 min ferry from Hiroshima

Matsuyama & Dogo Onsen

Shikoku's Spirited Away

Dogo Onsen Honkan is 3,000 years old and the bathhouse that inspired Spirited Away. It just finished a full restoration. The castle town around it has streetcar-accessible shopping arcades and Matsuyama Castle on the hill above. 70 minutes by ferry from Hiroshima, or JR from Okayama.

1–2 nights recommended Browse Stays
Ferry from Okayama

Naoshima & Art Islands

Contemporary Art in the Inland Sea

Tadao Ando's concrete museums built into hillsides. Yayoi Kusama's yellow pumpkin on the shore. Art installations in abandoned village homes across three islands. Chichu Art Museum holds Monet water lilies in a room designed so natural light shifts the painting throughout the day. Ferries from Uno Port (Okayama) or Takamatsu.

Northern Japan
