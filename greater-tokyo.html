Greater Tokyo

Onsen towns, ryokan nights, and volcanic lakes. All within 90 minutes of your Tokyo hotel.

The Second Trip

Your Base in Tokyo

Budget 4-5 days for this region. The JR Tokyo Wide Pass (¥10,180/3 days) covers round trips to Nikko, Hakone, Izu, and the Fuji Five Lakes, and usually pays for itself in one trip. Base hotels in Tokyo run around ¥10,000/night. The cost jump is the ryokan overnights: ¥15,000 for shared onsen, ¥25,000+ for private bath and kaiseki. Everything on this page is within 90 minutes of central Tokyo.

Stay Overnight

Where to Stay

Your base is central Tokyo. Overnight escapes and splurge nights, all 60–90 minutes by train.

Your base city

Tokyo

Your Greater Tokyo Hub

Tokyo has dozens of neighborhoods the tourist circuit skips: Shimokitazawa for vintage shops and live music, Yanaka for pre-war streetscapes and old-school kissaten, Koenji for ¥300 thrift stores and jazz bars. Business hotels run around ¥10,000/night across the city. This is your base for every day trip and overnight on this page.

Hakone's Quiet Side

Sengokuhara, Miyanoshita & the Old Tokaido

The splurge night. 85 minutes from Shinjuku on the Romancecar, and you're in a ryokan with a private outdoor bath. ¥15,000 gets the tatami room and shared onsen, ¥25,000+ adds the private bath and kaiseki dinner. Sengokuhara for the Open Air Museum and hiking trails. Miyanoshita for the historic ryokan strip. Day-trippers leave by 5pm; you stay for the evening soak.

2 hrs from Tokyo

Nikko & Kinugawa

The Overnight Nikko

Toshogu Shrine opens at 8am. Tour buses arrive at 10. Stay the night and you get two hours alone with Japan's most ornate shrine complex. Forest trails lead to Kanmangafuchi stone Buddhas, and Kinugawa Onsen is 20 minutes down the line. Yuba (tofu skin) is the local specialty, served everywhere in town.

2–2.5 hrs from Tokyo

The Quiet Side of Fuji

Saiko, Motosuko & Shizuoka Side

Motosuko has the Fuji view printed on the ¥1,000 bill. The western lakes (Saiko, Shojiko, Motosuko) give you the mountain at arm's length: lakeside camping, kayaks, forest walks through Aokigahara. Two hours from Shinjuku by direct bus.

2–3 hrs from Tokyo

The Izu Coast

Shimoda, Dogashima & the Eastern Shore

Black-sand beaches in Shimoda. Sea caves you boat through at Dogashima. Wasabi farms in the interior. Coastal ryokan with outdoor onsen right on the water. Kinmedai (golden-eye snapper) is the local fish, served everywhere along the coast. Two hours from Tokyo on the Odoriko express, and most foreign visitors have never heard of it.

2–3 nights recommended Browse Stays
3 hrs from Tokyo

Kusatsu Onsen

Japan's Best Hot Spring Town

Consistently voted Japan's #1 onsen by the Japanese themselves. The yubatake (hot water field) steams in the town center. Free public baths scattered through the streets. The water is so mineral-rich it changes the feel of your skin. Three hours from Tokyo, best in winter when the steam rises thick.
